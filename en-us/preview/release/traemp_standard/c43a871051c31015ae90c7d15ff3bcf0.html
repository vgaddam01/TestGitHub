Reserve Amtrak

Step 1: Enter the search criteria

  1. After you have selected Amtrak as the rail vendor, select one of the following types of trip options:

    • Round Trip

    • One Way

    • Multi City (if the configuration allows)

  2. Select the departure and arrival stations, days, and times. Concur supports train routes in the Northeast, Northwest, and Midwest regions.

  3. Click Search. The search results appear.

Step 2: Filter the results (if desired)

There are two sets of filters: one at the top of the page and one at the left side of the page.

Left side of the page: Use the up and down arrows (show and hide) as needed.

  • In the Change Search area, change your arrival or departure dates or times, if necessary. Click Search.

  • In the Rail Station Filters area, select the desired locations and/or change the number of stops.

Matrix - Top right side of the page: Directly above the search results is a grid that allows you to filter by the number of stops. Click the cell with the desired number of stops. The search results below will reflect that choice.

To see the rates in a different currency, use the currency converter in the upper-right corner.

Step 3: Sort the results (if desired)

Below the matrix is the Sorted By list. Select the desired sort option.

Step 4: Review the results

The search results appear on the Shop by Fares and Shop by Schedule tabs. Choose the desired tab. The fare, stations, times, and class appear.

To choose an outbound and return rail trip:

  1. Click the blue button for the desired leg of the trip. Then:

    • That segment "floats" to the Chosen Carriers area (above the Depart and Return tabs). Additional information is provided.

    • The Return tab is automatically displayed, showing the available return segments.

  2. Click the blue button for the desired return leg of the trip. It, too, moves to the Chosen Carriers area.

To move a selected segment back to the search results, either:

  • Click Remove. - or -

  • Click another segment from the search results. It replaces the existing segment in the Chosen Carriers area.

Step 5: Start the reservation process

  1. Review the various options, details, and rules to find the best trip.

  2. Click the blue "price" button. (Note that the shape and color of the icon to the left of the blue button reflects policy compliance.) The Review and Reserve Train page appears.

  3. Review the credit card information. Change it, if necessary.

  4. On the Review and Reserve Train page, you are advised of the available ticket delivery method(s) for the trip. This can include these, depending on the services offered at the station you depart from:
    • Quik-Trak (self-service ticketing kiosk)
    • Agent Counter, Metrolink (Los Angeles – San Diego only)
    • Ticket by Mail or FedEx
    Note

    The most likely scenario is the Quik-Trak method. In this case, all you have to do is swipe a valid credit card at the kiosk to confirm the trip and have the ticket printed.

  5. Review the information for accuracy, paying particular attention to any warnings.

  6. Click Next. The Travel Details page (itinerary) appears.

Step 6: Review the itinerary and book hotel, car, etc.

  1. Depending on your company's configuration, you may see links on the itinerary so that you can also add ground transportation, rental car, and/or hotel. Use as desired.

  2. Click Next. The Trip Booking Information page appears.

Step 7: Review the trip booking information

Use the Trip Booking Information page to enter additional information about your trip.

  1. Enter or modify your trip name. This is how the trip will appear on your itinerary and in the automated email from Concur.

  2. Enter a trip description, if needed.

  3. Enter any comments for the travel agent. Refer to your company policy for information regarding the use of the agent comments field.
    Note

    Be aware that making an entry in this field will likely generate an agency fee.

  4. Indicate if there is anyone else who should receive the initial confirmation email. Enter as many recipients as needed, separated by commas.

    • If you book the trip, you will automatically receive the email.

    • If you book as an arranger, you will receive the email as well.

  5. Choose your preferred email format, either plain text or HTML. Some companies do not offer travelers this option and always send plain text email.

  6. Your company may require billing information, for example, with a cost center field. If so, complete this information as indicated by your company.

  7. Click Next. Concur displays your itinerary a final time.

Step 8: Purchasing the tickets

  1. Review the information on the Trip Confirmation page, which includes any messages about ticketing policies.
    Note

    It is important to read the notes on this page as it will indicate how your company handles ticketing.

  2. Click Confirm Booking.
