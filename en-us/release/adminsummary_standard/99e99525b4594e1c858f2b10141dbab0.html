January 2017 Travel Standard Edition Admin Summary

Flight

Sabre - Remove Invalid Seat Selection, Finish Booking – United Airlines (UA) and/or American Airlines (AA)

Starting with the January release, if a user is assigned to a premium seat but does not qualify for a premium seat, the seat will be cancelled and the reservations (PNR) will be created without the seat assignment. The user will see a message on the itinerary: We were unable to confirm a seat assignment. Click on "Selected Seat" to view the seat map and manually select your seat.

User/Customer Benefit: Bookings will no longer fail and customers will now be able to secure the booking and desired fare without a delay.

Sabre - Support for Flex Fares in the Intra-New Zealand Market

Historically, Concur has supported the branded fares in the Australian and TransTasman markets, through the Flex Faring functionality, but we have not be able to support the intra-New Zealand market due to some technical limitations with how fares are filed in that market. With a recent change by Air New Zealand (NZ) in how some branded fares are filed, Concur will now be able to support those fares through our Flex Faring functionality.

User/Customer Benefit: This change provides additional traveller options.

Apollo/Galileo - Branded Fares Support

Concur is pleased to announce support for branded fares for our Apollo and Galileo customers for a specific group of airlines: Delta Air Lines (DL), Virgin America (VX), Germanwings (4U), Eurowings (EW), Vueling Airlines (VY), Binter Canarias (NT), Cathay Pacific (CX), Singapore Airlines (SQ), and United Airlines (UA) (United’s fares may not be available immediately, but Concur Travel should automatically start displaying them as soon as they are made available in Apollo or Galileo.)

Note This feature will be made available shortly after the release.

User/Customer Benefit: With multiple fare options in each booking class, travelers will be able to enjoy more options when booking their flights. The new fares will reflect fixed services included in the price as well as different rebooking and refund conditions. Passengers can thus always choose the exact fare that best meets their needs and pay only for the services they actually want to use.

Amadeus - Change Seat Handling for Porter Airlines

Concur has received reports from our Amadeus customers that Concur Travel is confirming seat requests for Porter Airlines’ Firm fares, which do not include a pre-assigned seat. Concur has stopped this practice.

User/Customer Benefit: This change provides a better user experience for travelers.

Web Fares

Travelfusion – Add "Tour Code" for Avianca Brazil

With the October 2016 release, we announced the availability of Avianca Brazil in Concur via Travelfusion as a B2B carrier. In order to support corporate discounts and promotional fares, Avianca requires the additional Tour Code parameter. With this release, we support the required parameter.

User/Customer Benefit: Adding a Tour Code to the B2B section allows access to discounted promotional or corporate fares.

Southwest Migration to New Reservation System and the Booking Impact

Southwest Airlines is migrating their reservation system from their existing Sabre-based platform to an Amadeus Altéa platform. Southwest has announced that the final cutover date will be May 8 2017. Neither Southwest nor Concur can manage one PNR in two different backend reservation systems.

Note the following: Travel in Concur Travel via Southwest Direct Connect is unaffected if it is:

  • Initiated and completed before May 8 (like both outbound and return are before May 8) – or –

  • Initiated and completed on or after May 8 (like both outbound and return are on or after May 8)

If a user/arranger attempts to book travel in Concur Travel via Southwest Direct Connect that includes or traverses the May 8 date (like the return trip is on or after May 8):

  • An error message appears, indicating that the trip cannot be booked.

  • Flights that include or traverse the May 8 date must be booked in two separate transactions in Concur or directly at https://www.southwest.com/.

A user/arranger cannot change a trip in Concur Travel via Southwest Direct Connect so that the new trip includes or traverses the May 8 date. The original trip must be cancelled/refunded and then re-booked separately.

Train

**COMING SOON** Renfe and Swedish Rail Available via Concur Travel

Concur Travel currently provides rail content for US, Canada, France, Germany, and the United Kingdom. Concur will add content for Spain (Renfe) and Sweden to the portfolio of Concur Travel rail carriers. We are targeting end of the first calendar quarter of 2017 to have the content available.

User/Customer Benefit: Travelers can book rail content for Spain and Sweden in Concur Travel – a single, intuitive, web-based interface with effortless user experience.

Profile

XML Profile Sync Moves to Deprecated State January 1, 2017

Starting in October 2016, XML Profile Sync moved into a deprecated state. Moving to a deprecated state means that Concur will address only mission-critical issues between January and June 2017. After June 2017, Concur will no longer support XML Profile Sync as a way to synchronize profile data with Concur.

User/Customer Benefit: Using the Travel Profile API 2.0 provides a better experience.

Emerald Club – National / Enterprise Now Superchain

Enterprise Holdings has requested that a superchain be created in order to consolidate both Enterprise and National brands under a new umbrella called Emerald Club – National/Enterprise.

User/Customer Benefit: Emerald Club will allow loyalty benefits for both National and Enterprise, which provides a better user experience for travelers.

Miscellaneous

Clarification about Currency Conversion

As noted in the September 2016 release notes, the user/arranger can use the currency list in the upper-right corner to convert fares/rates to a different currency. One thing to note: Concur uses the OANDA rate service when calculating the conversion from one currency to another. The vendor (in this case, Hertz) may use a different rate service. So, after the user selects (in this case) a car and the actual fare/rate appears on the Review and Reserve page, there may be a slight difference in the fare/rate due to the difference in conversion rates.

**REMINDER** Change in the Monthly Release Schedule

As shown on the 2017 release calendar, starting in March, Travel releases will occur on Saturday instead of Thursday. So, instead of seeing release-related changes on Friday of release week, they will appear to users on Monday (or over the weekend, if users sign in to Concur). The release calendar is available here: http://www.concurtraining.com/customers/tech_pubs/ReleaseCalendar/_ReleaseCalendar_client.htm (English Only)

**COMING SOON** Support for TLS v 1.0 Encryption Protocol to End March of 2017

Concur announces an End of Support cycle for version 1.0 of the TLS encryption protocol, continuing support for the more secure 1.1 and 1.2 versions of TLS. As background, the TLS protocol allows secure back and forth communications between a phone or computer and a cloud-based service. Note that, since less than 1% of Concur users are logging in to Concur Solutions using browsers or phones limited to version 1.0, a very small number of users are affected by this support discontinuation.
